Edinburgh Street Photography

About Robert Bloomfield

  • 1938 - was born in Leeds (UK)to freelance journalist Mary (née Gough) and George Blomfield, a surgeon and engineer, and an amateur photographer
  • 1956 - aged 18, he moved to Scotland to study medicine at the University of Edinburgh, where he remained until 1967
  • Blomfield practised as a family doctor in Wrexham then Hebden Bridge.
  • He made street photography all over the UK from the late 1950s in Edinburgh, through to the early 1970s
  • 1965 - he met Jane Alexander, an art historian working at the Courtauld Institute.
  • 1975 - They married, and had three sons, William, George and Edward
  • 1999 - Robert suffered a stroke, which forced him to hang up his beloved but heavy Nikons. Switching to a small digital camera, he continued to snap away at everything around him
  • 2018 - he had an exhibition of work made in Edinburgh at the City Art Centre there
  • 2020 - he passed away just a few short weeks after he had realised his long held ambition to have a book of his photos published
